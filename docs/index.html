What's inside

Grab it and drag

Select a face and push/pull it, resize with handles, snap to a grid, group objects and move them together. Direct and immediate — no menu-diving.

A real CAD kernel

Built on OpenCASCADE — the engine behind FreeCAD. True B-rep solids with exact faces, edges, fillets and booleans. Real geometry, not meshes.

Everything is parametric

Every action lives in a history you can re-open, tweak, re-order, or roll back. Change an early step and the model rebuilds. Nothing is baked.

A proper sketcher

A real pen and Bézier tool with dimensional constraints drives extrudes and revolves. Sketch the intent; let the solver hold it.

Print-ready output

Export watertight STLs straight to your slicer — and save the whole parametric project, not a flattened mesh you can never edit again.

Free & open source

Released under MPL 2.0 — yours to use, study, and build on. No account, no telemetry, no cost. Community-driven.

Early days, industrial foundations

Cadette is an early, single-developer build, so it's lean on features for now — there's no STL import or sweep yet. But the foundation isn't a toy: it's the same industrial-grade B-rep kernel that serious CAD is built on. The plan is to keep the directness while the capability grows.

Cadette is meant to be the calm voice in your headset — encouraging, precise, and never above explaining the basics.
